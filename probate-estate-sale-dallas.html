Probate & Estate Sale Questions — Answered

Does the property have to go through probate before we can sell?
In most cases, yes — unless the property was held in a living trust or with a transfer-on-death deed. Texas does allow a process called "muniment of title" for simple estates, which can be faster. Your probate attorney can advise on the right path for your situation.
How long does probate take in Texas?
A straightforward independent administration typically takes 6–12 months from filing to completion. Complex estates with disputes or contested wills can take longer. We're patient — we'll go under contract now and close when the court approves.
What if there are back taxes or a mortgage on the property?
Common in estate properties. All outstanding debts — taxes, mortgage balances, HOA fees, liens — are paid from the sale proceeds at closing. The estate and heirs receive whatever remains after payoff.
Do we need to do anything to the property before selling?
Nothing. You take whatever personal belongings you want to keep. Leave the rest — furniture, appliances, personal items, decades of belongings. We buy the property as-is and handle the cleanout ourselves.
We have 4 heirs. Can you work with all of us?
Yes. We work with multi-heir estates regularly. We can accommodate separate signing appointments if needed, and the title company distributes proceeds according to each heir's share at closing.

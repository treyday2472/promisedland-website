Rental Property Sale — Questions Answered

Do I have to notify my tenants before selling?
Texas law requires reasonable notice for property entry and inspections. You must also notify tenants of the new owner at closing. We handle these notifications as part of the transaction — you don't need to do it yourself.
What happens to the tenant's security deposit?
At closing, the security deposit transfers to us as the new owner. This is documented in the closing paperwork. You're released from all liability related to the deposit once it's transferred.
What if I have a tenant who refuses to let anyone in for an inspection?
We can often make an offer based on the outside of the property, comparable sales, and a walk-through when the tenant is not an issue. We've bought properties sight-unseen from the interior before.
What if there's an active eviction in progress?
We can still buy. We'll factor the eviction status into the offer and take over the process at closing. You don't have to see it through to completion before selling.
Will I owe taxes on the sale of a rental property?
Possibly. Capital gains taxes and depreciation recapture may apply to rental property sales. We'd recommend speaking with a CPA before closing. We're real estate investors, not tax advisors.

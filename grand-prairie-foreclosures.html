Grand Prairie Foreclosure — Common Questions

What does a trustee sale notice mean in Grand Prairie?
It means a lender has filed public notice that they intend to foreclose on a property. In Texas, the sale is scheduled for the first Tuesday of the following month after a 21-day posting period. The homeowner still has time to act before the auction.
Can I sell my Grand Prairie house if it is in foreclosure?
Yes. As long as the foreclosure sale has not happened yet, you can sell your home, pay off the lender, and potentially walk away with remaining equity. Cash buyers like Promised Land Property Solutions can close in as few as 7 days.
Does Grand Prairie fall under Dallas County or Tarrant County for foreclosures?
Grand Prairie is a split-county city. Properties east of Highway 360 generally fall under Dallas County, while properties west of Highway 360 fall under Tarrant County. If you are not sure which county your Grand Prairie property falls under, check both the Dallas County Clerk and Tarrant County Clerk trustee sale notice portals.
Where does this Grand Prairie foreclosure data come from?
All records on this page are sourced from Dallas County public filings — specifically trustee sale notices published by the Dallas County Clerk. This page covers Dallas County records only. Grand Prairie properties in Tarrant County would appear in Tarrant County records.

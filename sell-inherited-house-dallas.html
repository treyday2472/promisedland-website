Inherited House Questions — Answered

Do I need to go through probate before selling in Texas?
In most cases, yes. If the deceased did not have a living trust or transfer-on-death deed, the property will likely need to go through probate before it can be sold. We can be ready to close as soon as probate court approves the sale.
How long does probate take in Texas?
A straightforward Texas probate typically takes 6–12 months. Complex estates can take longer. The good news is that Texas allows "muniment of title" in some cases, which is much faster.
What if there are multiple heirs who need to agree?
All heirs with a legal interest in the property typically need to agree to the sale. We're experienced working with multiple heirs and can help facilitate the process so everyone is satisfied.
Do I need to clean out the house first?
No. We buy inherited properties with everything in them — furniture, belongings, decades of accumulated items. You take what you want and leave the rest to us.
What if the property has back taxes or liens?
That's common with inherited properties. We can factor this into the offer and handle paying off liens and back taxes at closing so you don't have to deal with it separately.

Dallas Foreclosure — Common Questions

What does a trustee sale notice mean?
It means a lender has filed public notice that they intend to foreclose on a property. In Texas, the sale is scheduled for the first Tuesday of the following month, after a 21-day posting period. The homeowner still has time to act before the auction.
How fast can you close in Dallas?
In most cases we can close in 7–14 days. If you have an upcoming foreclosure sale date, tell us and we'll prioritize your case to close before it hits.
Do I need to make repairs before selling?
No. We buy houses as-is, in any condition. You don't need to clean, repair, or stage anything.
Will I owe any fees or commissions?
No agent commissions. No closing fees paid by you. We cover the costs and make the process as simple as possible.
Where does this foreclosure data come from?
All records are sourced from Dallas County public filings — specifically trustee sale notices published by the Dallas County Clerk. These are public court records available to anyone.

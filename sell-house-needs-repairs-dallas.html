Why Repairing Before You Sell Often Doesn't Pay Off

Before spending money on repairs, run the numbers. Most sellers don't come out ahead.

🔨

Contractor Costs Add Up

A roof replacement runs $10,000–$20,000. Foundation work can exceed $30,000. Kitchen and bath updates easily hit $25,000+. The final repair bill is almost always higher than the initial estimate.

📅

Repairs Take Months

Contractors are booked out. Permits take time. While you wait, you're still paying mortgage, insurance, property taxes, and utilities on a house you're trying to sell.

📉

Buyers Still Negotiate Down

Even after you fix everything, buyers will hire inspectors who find more issues. Then they use those findings to negotiate your price down — after you've already spent the money.

💵

Selling As-Is Skips All of That

We buy the house in its current condition. No repairs, no inspections used against you, no months of waiting. You get a cash offer and close on your timeline.

Selling a Fixer-Upper — Questions Answered

Do I need to disclose all the problems with the house?
Yes, Texas law requires sellers to disclose known material defects. But unlike a traditional sale where disclosures scare off buyers, we expect the issues. Just tell us what you know and we'll factor it in honestly.
Will you still make an offer if the house has foundation issues?
Yes. Foundation issues are one of the most common things we deal with in Dallas. We work with contractors who specialize in foundation repair and we factor those costs into our offer.
Do I need to clean out the house?
No. Leave whatever you don't want. Old furniture, appliances, junk — we handle the cleanout. Take what matters to you and leave the rest.
How is your offer calculated?
We look at the after-repair value of the property (what it would sell for fixed up), subtract the estimated repair costs, our holding costs, and a modest profit margin. The result is your offer. We're transparent about how we get there.
What if the house has code violations or unpermitted work?
We buy houses with code violations and unpermitted additions regularly. These are issues we handle after closing — you don't need to resolve them before you sell.

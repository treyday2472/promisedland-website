Divorce Home Sale Questions — Answered

What if my spouse and I can't agree on anything — can we still sell?
If you're in active disagreement, a court may need to order the sale. However, a cash sale is often proposed by mediators and attorneys precisely because it's the fastest way to separate the asset. We work with attorneys and mediators regularly.
Who gets the proceeds from the sale?
That's determined by your divorce settlement or court order. At closing, proceeds are distributed according to your agreement — to each party directly, to attorneys, or held in escrow pending a ruling.
Can one spouse sell the house without the other's consent?
In Texas, both spouses typically must consent to selling marital property. However, a court can order the sale. We're experienced in these situations and can move quickly once legal clearance is in place.
What if there's still a mortgage on the house?
The mortgage balance is paid off from the sale proceeds at closing. Both parties receive whatever is left after the payoff. If the balance is more than the value, we can discuss options including a short sale.
Do we both need to be present at closing?
In most cases, both parties need to sign. We can coordinate separate signing appointments or remote signings if needed so you don't have to be in the same room.
